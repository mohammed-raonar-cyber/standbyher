What We Stand For

More Than Hygiene.
It's About Dignity.

Stand By Her began with a simple belief — that every woman deserves access to menstrual hygiene products and the knowledge to use them safely. But over five years, we've learned it's about far more than that.

It's about dismantling the shame that surrounds periods. It's about reaching the women in Bengaluru's most underserved communities — where conversations about menstruation are still taboo, and where access to basic hygiene is a luxury not everyone can afford.

This initiative directly aligns with UN SDG 3 (Good Health & Well-being) and UN SDG 5 (Gender Equality).

Every woman who knows her rights moves society forward.

What Happens on June 7th

A day built to educate, empower, and erase stigma — at Andrahalli Government School, Bengaluru.

01

Awareness Sessions

Interactive sessions on menstrual health, correct usage and disposal of sanitary products, and nutrition during menstruation — led by volunteers and community health advocates.

02

Pad Distribution

Every woman attending receives a year-long supply of sanitary pads — sourced from Freedom Fly Multi Products, a Gujarat-based ISO 9001:2015 certified social enterprise.

03

Myth Busting

We tackle stigma head-on — addressing taboos, misconceptions, and cultural silences that prevent women from accessing proper care and talking openly about their health.

04

Open Conversations

A judgment-free space for women and girls to ask questions, share experiences, and feel heard — because awareness starts with conversation.

Our Journey

Five Editions.
One Growing Movement.

2022
Edition I · 2022
The Beginning

Stand By Her launched its very first edition at Andrahalli, Bengaluru — a small but purposeful event that planted the seeds of a movement. A handful of AIESEC members, a community that needed to be heard, and a conversation that needed to begin.

2023
Edition II · 2023
Growing Roots

The second edition expanded outreach and deepened community trust. More women attended, more conversations were had, and the initiative began to build a reputation as a consistent, caring presence in the community.

2024
Edition III · 2024
Deepening Impact

With stronger partnerships and a growing team, the 2024 edition scaled its reach, improved its educational content, and brought in more community leaders. Stand By Her was no longer just an event — it was a movement.

2025
Edition IV · 2025
Record Edition
Historic Scale

Over 150 women and girls attended — the largest edition in four years. 9,000+ sanitary pads were distributed, providing every attendee with a full year's supply. ₹1.8 lakhs raised. The DIG South, Bengaluru Police (Mr. Basava Raj Zille) and retired SBI General Manager Mr. K.M. Mamdi graced the event, endorsing the cause and calling for citywide replication. Partnered with Freedom Fly Multi Products — a Gujarat-based pad-making social enterprise.

About AIESEC

Youth-Led.
Impact-Driven.

AIESEC is a global youth-led organisation present in over 100 countries and territories. We develop leadership in young people through practical, real-world experiences — volunteering, international exchanges, and community-driven initiatives.

AIESEC in Bengaluru runs Stand By Her as part of our commitment to creating meaningful, lasting social impact in our city. Every aspect of this initiative — from planning to execution — is led by students and young professionals who believe in the power of purposeful action.
